What is Ashtanga Yoga ?

Ashtanga yoga is a dynamic, flowing style that connects the movement of the body with the breath.
The method stresses the importance of daily practice of a set series of movements.
There are six series of Ashtanga yoga sequences, which the student progresses through at their own pace.

Benefits of Ashtanga Yoga

Improves Coordination

• Ashtanga Yoga is a powerful tool for adjusting the body.
It helps in improving concentration, balance and coordination.
A fast and intensive asana set gives you a better sense of rhythm and increases your awareness of movement and flow in your body.

Helps in Toning Muscles

• All this increased strength is possible because you are building muscles throughout your body during these movements.
Yoga does give you abs, it is true! Once you begin doing it regularly, you can observe the difference in your arms, shoulders, abdomen as well as calves.

Reduces Back Pain

• The Ashtanga Primary Series includes postures that stretch and strengthen the lower back muscles.
And because the same postures are performed every time you step on the mat, keeping those muscles flexed (and strengthened) will help reduce lower back pain.

Controls Weight

• Do you know the result of performing cardio as well as building muscles? You burn fat!
Since you get committed to a daily Ashtanga practice, you will notice less fat on arms and around the stomach area, so if you want to shed a few pounds, then this is the style of yoga for you.

Reduces Risk of Injuries

• Ashtanga develops a strong, flexible, and much less injury-prone body in a safe and conscious way, which means it can be a great addition to other sports.
As you age, your body becomes more prone to injury.
And practicing yoga is a great way to stay fit and flexible so you can enjoy a better quality of life for longer.

Lowers Blood Pressure

• In Ashtanga Yoga, we practice a breathing technique known as Ujjayi.
The glottis in the back of the throat is pulled together slightly to create the sound of the ocean when you breathe in and out through your nose.
Lengthen your inhalations and exhalations to create long, deep, calm breaths.
Many studies suggest that this breathing technique lowers blood pressure over time.
