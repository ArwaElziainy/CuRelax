What is Over Thinking ?

Nowadays we, especially the new generations are faced with a problem of excessive thinking, what is often referred to as overthinking.
By classical definition overthinking is thought process that consumes mental power (energy) and does not add any additional value.
Overthinking is a psychological condition in which the minds tends to think too much, and worry too much, about some topics that either affected a person in the past, or will affect a person in the future.

Impact of Over Thinking

Physical Impact

• Some of patients who deal with negative thoughts and anxiety have also experienced headaches, body aches and stomach problems.

Mental health Impact

• Overthinking leads to exhausting the brain cells, thus making them incapable of generating creative insights.
• Disrupts daily routine activities.
Chronic overthinking will take up much of your day and at the end you will have little productivity to show.

Cognitive Imapct

• It decreases self-confidence and fosters self-doubt.
Increased tendency to overthink leads to increased self-doubt which again reinforces the overthinking cognitive style.

Behavioral Imapct

• The truth is overthinking can affect anything and everything about your life. It can affect the way you work with others, impact your social life, and take a toll on your personal life.
What that means is it can start to wear away at you and at the relationships you have with the people around you.
Overthinking can create serious problems in your life.

How to Treat From Over Thinking ?

Meditation Sessions

• It can help you in sets your perspective right , Helps you overcome negative thoughts , Declutters your mind and Frees you from attachment.

Exercise regularly (physical works)

• It is a good, constructive habit that can help you relieve any stress and anger that might have built.
You will improve both your physical and psychological health and have a healthier alternative to clear your head when negative thoughts start invading.

Spend more time in the present moment

• Overthinking usually sets us in the past or the future.
When your mind goes into overdrive, you need to slow down and be more aware of your body and your surroundings.
Train your mind to stop wandering and bring your focus on what is happening in the present moment.

Travelling

• Travelling is a great way to change a direction of thinking.
New places, new pathways. During your travel time different ports in your brain open up.
If you are stuck at negative spiraling thought patterns, planning a journey might be your answer.
